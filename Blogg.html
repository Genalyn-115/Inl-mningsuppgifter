Live From Your Heart

Living from your heart means that you choose a life and a lifestyle that are true for you and your family. It means you make important decisions because they resonate with your heart and your own values, and not necessarily with those of others. Living from your heart means that you trust your own instincts more than the pressures from advertising or the expectations of society, neighbors and friends. Living from your heart, however does not mean you become a rebel, break family tradition or become different from everyone else. It’s far more subtle than that. Living from the heart is about trusting that quiet voice within you that emerges when you quiet down enough to listen. It’s that voice that speaks to you from a place of wisdom and common sense instead of from frantic chatter of habit. When you trust your heart rather than your habits, new insights will pop into your mind. These insights can be the realization of the necessity to break a destructive habit, to an answer of how you communicate differently with someone you love. You might also have insights about who you choose to spend time as well as new ways to solve problems. It all starts from listening to your heart.
